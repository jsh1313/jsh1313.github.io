DIM SUM

What is Dim Sum? Well, Dim Sum is a large range of small dishes that originally had its origin from Chinese cuisine. However, any Dim Sum dishes today are actually associated with Cantonese cuisine, more spcifically Hong Kong. Due to its Cantonese association, the name "Dim Sum" is actually the Cantonese pronunciation.

Here are some popular Dim Sum dishes!

Shrimp Dumpling 蝦餃
Shrimp Dumplings have a smooth and clear skin with only shrimp as the filling. It has a very chewy texture.

Shumai 燒賣
Shumai are steamed dumplings with pork and prawns, usually topped off with crab roe and mushroom.

Haam Seui Gok 鹹水角
Haam Seui Gok are deep fried dumplings with a slightly savory filling of pork and chopped vegetables in a sweet and sticky wrapping.

Tofu Skin Roll 腐皮捲
Tofu Skin Roll is a roll made of tofu skin filled with various meats and sliced vegetables.

Turnip Cake 蘿蔔糕
Turnip Cake is pudding made from a mix of shredded white radish, bits of dried shrimp, Chinese sausage and mushroom that is steamed, sliced, and pan-fried.

Phoenix Claws 鳳爪
Phoenix Claws are steamed chicken feet that have been deep fried and boiled with douchi.

Egg Tarts 蛋撻
Egg Tarts are baked tarts with egg custard filling.

These are only some of many dishes in Dim Sum! Try it out to see which ones you like better. Remember, everyone's taste is different so please do not judge others based on their favorite dish!

Enjoy!!!
